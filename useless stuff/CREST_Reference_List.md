## Citation Guidance

### When writing the Literature Review:
Do not write: *"Mohanty et al. (2016) used deep learning to detect plant disease."*
Do write: *"Mohanty et al. (2016) demonstrated that deep convolutional networks can classify plant disease from leaf images with 99.35% accuracy when tested on the controlled PlantVillage dataset, though this figure has been criticised as unrealistically high for field deployment conditions (Barbedo, 2019; Ramcharan et al., 2017)."*

The difference: synthesis plus critical evaluation vs. description.

### When writing the Methodology:
Cite every architectural decision: *"...following the two-phase fine-tuning strategy described by Yosinski et al. (2014)..."*
Cite the dataset: *"...the PlantVillage dataset (Mohanty et al., 2016), comprising 54,306 leaf images across 38 disease classes..."*
Cite Grad-CAM: *"...Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM; Selvaraju et al., 2017) was applied to visualise..."*
